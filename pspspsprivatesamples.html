These are some samples about application of our games on rehabilitation and trainning.

This is an aplication of Virtual Reality (VR) game and Transcranial Direct Current Stimulation (tDCS) in an individual with CEREBRAL PALSY. We use tDCS during tasks in VR to modulate cortical excitability, producing facilitatory or inhibitory effects upon a variety of behaviors. The combination of these technologies has shown excellent results.

We can use VR games to evaluate and improve performance. In this example we have an Olympic level wheelchair basketball player trying to hit the balls at the exact moment they cross the targets. As he performs this pre-prepared task, we track details of precision and accuracy of each target with milliseconds of sensitivity, something that only the human eye can't detect.

Tasks performed in serious games in immersive or non-immersive virtual environment impact performance in tasks performed in real life.

This is Ibis, physioterepist and part of our team, testing an amazing technology of tracking movements. She is at Exerter university as part of her PhD.
